Pill

A control for choosing among a set of options — you click it to pick/toggle a value, either on a single item (a modal picker) or across your whole collection (a sidebar filter).

How it differs from Badge/Chip/Stat: 20px radius, 1.5px border, interactive (click to toggle), font 700/.04em/uppercase/Rajdhani, padding .25rem .65rem. Unlike Badge (no border, never interactive) and Chip (99px radius, has its own remove/action button rather than being one of several options), a Pill is always part of a group of mutually-relevant choices.
Used in: Add/Edit modal, Add-to-Collection modal, sidebar filters, purchase editor (side panel).
Selector Pill
Purpose: assign a value to one item. Why its own subcategory: stays colorless (transparent background, muted border) until you pick a value — no count, since it's not summarizing anything across the collection.

Filter Pill
Purpose: filter your whole collection by a value. Why its own subcategory: stays tinted with its color at all times (dims to 55% opacity when not selected, rather than going transparent) so you can scan the full palette of options at a glance, and always shows a live count of how many games match — the count sits in its own small contrasting sub-chip so it stays legible regardless of the pill's own color.

Badge

A static, read-only label — displays a value or status. Nothing to click, nothing to choose.

How it differs from Pill/Chip/Stat: 4px radius, no border, always solid fill, non-interactive, font 700/.56rem/.04em/uppercase, padding 2px 6px, line-height 1.4 — identical across every badge in the app regardless of what data it shows; only its background/text color changes. Unlike Pill it's never part of a "pick one" group, and unlike Chip it has no remove button or action.
Used on: game cards (grid + list), collection cards, side panel, purchase editor.

No subcategories — every badge in the app shares the exact same shape/typography, varying only by color and text.

Chip

A standalone item that isn't part of a "pick one of many" group — it carries its own action (remove it, or trigger something) rather than being one of several options you choose between.

How it differs from Pill/Badge/Stat: 99px radius (fully rounded capsule), 1px border, has its own action baked into the element itself (an × to remove it, or a click to trigger a retry) — unlike Pill, it's not part of a group of alternatives; unlike Badge, it's interactive.
Used in: sidebar (active filters), Add/Edit modal (tag inputs), mobile floating status.

Purpose: represent one currently-applied filter. Why its own subcategory: shows a Label: Value pair (not just a bare value), since it needs to say which filter this is as well as what it's set to.

Status Chip
Purpose: show an app-level status, not tied to a specific game. Why its own subcategory: no remove action at all — instead it's conditionally clickable (only in its error/offline state) to trigger a retry. Stat

A plain-text, aggregate page-level readout — not a status on any one item.

How it differs from Pill/Badge/Chip: no box at all — no radius, no border, no background/fill, not interactive. This is deliberate: Pill, Badge, and Chip are all "a labeled box" of some kind; Stat is the one type that's just text, which is what visually marks it as a summary rather than a per-item indicator.
Used in: header, sidebar stats row.

No subcategories — one plain-text treatment used everywhere it appears.
